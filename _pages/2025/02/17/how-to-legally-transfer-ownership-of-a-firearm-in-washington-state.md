Transferring firearm ownership in Washington State requires adherence to specific legal procedures to ensure compliance with state laws. This guide outlines the necessary steps and provides relevant legal references.

## **1. Summary: Steps to Transfer Firearm Ownership in Washington State**

1. **Find a Licensed Dealer (FFL)** – All firearm transfers must go through a licensed firearms dealer, except for certain exemptions like transfers between immediate family members.
2. **Meet In-Person with the Dealer** – Both the current owner (transferor) and the recipient (transferee) must visit the FFL together to initiate the transfer.
3. **Complete Required Paperwork** – The recipient must fill out ATF Form 4473, which includes personal details, background information, and firearm details. A valid government-issued ID is required.
4. **Background Check** – The FFL submits the recipient’s information for a background check to ensure they are legally eligible to own a firearm.
5. **Waiting Period** – If transferring a pistol or semiautomatic assault rifle, the recipient must wait up to 10 business days before taking possession. Other firearms can be transferred once the background check is approved.
6. **Pay Any Applicable Fees** – The FFL may charge a transfer fee for processing the paperwork and conducting the background check.
7. **Receive the Firearm** – Once all legal requirements are met, the firearm is transferred to the new owner, and the FFL keeps a record of the transaction.

Final Notes:

Always verify current laws before transferring a firearm, as regulations may change. Check with local authorities and the Washington State Department of Licensing for updated legal requirements.

## **2. Background Check Requirement**

In Washington State, all firearm transfers, including private sales and gifts, must be conducted through a licensed firearms dealer (FFL). The dealer will perform a background check on the recipient to ensure they are eligible to possess a firearm.

*Legal Reference:*
- Revised Code of Washington (RCW) 9.41.113: [Background checks for firearm sales or transfers](https://app.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=9.41.113)

## **3. In-Person Meeting with Licensed Dealer**

Both the current owner (transferor) and the recipient (transferee) must be present at the FFL's premises to initiate the transfer process. The dealer will facilitate the necessary paperwork and background check.

## **4. Completion of Required Forms**

The recipient must complete **Form 4473**, the Firearms Transaction Record, which is required for all firearm transfers. The form asks for personal information, explained as follows.

**Personal Information:**

- Full name, current address, and date of birth.
- Gender, place of birth.
- Country of citizenship.
- Race and ethnicity (optional).
- Social Security Number (optional, but aids in background checks).
- Information on whether the recipient has ever been convicted of a felony or misdemeanor, has a restraining order, or has been adjudicated as mentally defective.
- Details about the firearm being transferred (e.g., make, model, serial number).

**Identification Details:**

- Type of government-issued photo identification provided (e.g., driver's license)
- ID number
- Issuing authority

**Background Information:**

- Responses to questions regarding criminal history
- History of substance abuse
- Mental health history
- Citizenship status
- Any restraining orders
- Other factors that may prohibit firearm possession
  
The form also includes a series of questions to determine the eligibility of the recipient to own a firearm, such as criminal history, restraining orders, and mental health status.

You can access **Form 4473** directly from the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives (ATF) website here: [ATF Form 4473](https://www.atf.gov/file/108736/download).

For detailed instructions on completing the form, review the guidelines provided by the ATF, available on the [ATF Webpage for Form 4473](https://www.atf.gov/firearms/atf-form-4473-firearms-transaction-record-revisions).

Note that **both the guidelines and the form itself are subject to change at any time**, and the information provided in this guide may not reflect the most up-to-date information regarding firearm transfers and Form 4473.

Once completed, the dealer will process the background check and proceed with the transfer if the recipient passes the check.

## **5. Waiting Period**

Washington State enforces a waiting period before the firearm can be transferred to the recipient. The waiting period is:

- **Pistols and Semiautomatic Assault Rifles:** Up to 10 business days.
- **Other Firearms:** The transfer can proceed once the background check is completed and approved.

*Legal Reference:*
- RCW 9.41.092: [Waiting period for delivery of pistol or semiautomatic assault rifle](https://app.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=9.41.092)

## **6. Exceptions to the Transfer Requirement**

Certain transfers are exempt from the requirement to go through an FFL, including:

- Transfers between immediate family members (spouses, domestic partners, parents, children, siblings).
- Transfers of antique firearms.
- Temporary transfers to prevent imminent death or great bodily harm.

*Legal Reference:*
- RCW 9.41.113(4): [Exceptions to background check requirement](https://app.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=9.41.113)

## **7. Fees**

The FFL may charge a fee for facilitating the transfer, which covers the background check and administrative costs. Fees can vary, so it's advisable to inquire with the dealer beforehand.

## **8. Record Keeping**

The FFL is required to maintain records of the transfer as per federal and state regulations. It's recommended that both parties retain copies of the transfer documentation for their personal records.

## **Important Considerations:**

- Ensure that the recipient is legally eligible to possess a firearm under both federal and state law.
- Comply with all local ordinances and regulations that may affect firearm transfers.
- For transfers involving concealed pistol licenses, additional requirements may apply.
